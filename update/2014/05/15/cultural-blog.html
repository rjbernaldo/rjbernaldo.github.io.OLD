Affirmation and Stereotype Threat

How do you feel when you think about your values?

I feel encouraged to practice this throughout my entire life. Even though I am about to learn with people with a totally different culture, I believe being kind and understanding is a general value that can be practiced no matter where I am, or who I am talking to.

Do you think it can help you mediate stereotype threat?

Of course. I believe extending my patience to understand each individual I work with can mediate this threat completely. The stereotype threat that came up on my last week's blog was actually about someone who was not from my country, so I now know how diverse each person is.

Can you think of any other exercises you can use to help you when you are feeling down at DBC?

One rule I have been practicing all my life is just simply stepping back to get a different perspective on the situation. I have my point of view, but what is the other person's point of view? It is important to always put your shoes in the person you are working with so you can understand where he is coming from, and why his or her actions are such.

I have learned that each person is uniquely molded by his environment and community. I should remember this to be able to be an effective contributor to the world's tech industry.

Rj Bernaldo
